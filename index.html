About Us

The Food Closet is a grocery program located at the Covenant Presbyterian Church in Room 9. This program is designed to help needy families with children or disabilities by assisting them in weekly food needs. It serves families who live in Palo Alto whose income is less than 150% of the federal poverty level. Families who come to the Food Closet may need help for a short or a long time.

To receive food from the South Palo Alto Food Closet, you must receive a referral from one of our referring organizations. Families with children may receive food once a week and couples every other week. Single persons are not typically accepted, and are referred instead to the larger Palo Alto Food Closet on University Avenue.

Volunteers are the heart and soul of our organization

The Food Closet relies on volunteers. We are completely staffed by volunteers. Some are affiliated with the referring organizations and some are unaffiliated but want to help those in need in their own community. Financial support comes from some, if not all, of the organizations as well as from individuals.

Volunteering at the Food Closet is a tangible contribution to the Palo Alto community. We welcome any help you are willing to provide. Please contact us and we'd be happy to help you get started.

Interested in volunteering at the Food Closet? Here are some things we could use your help with:

  • Sorting groceries
  • Distributing groceries
  • Picking up donated items
  • Cleaning the Food Closet
  • Speaking to interested groups
  • Organizing a food drive
Please help us keep our refrigerators and shelves stocked

We welcome and gratefully accept any and all food donations you wish to make to the Food Closet. If you would like to donate, please contact us and we will help arrange a good time for you to stop by.
